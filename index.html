Income Management

The 50/30/20 rule helps manage income effectively.

50% Needs: Essential expenses like rent, food, and transportation.
30% Wants: Non-essential spending on leisure and entertainment.
20% Savings: Investments, debt repayment, and emergency funds.

Building a Financial Portfolio

A financial portfolio is key to financial security.

Savings: Start saving early and consistently to build a financial safety net for future goals and unexpected expenses.
Investments: Invest smartly in stocks, mutual funds, or real estate to grow wealth and create passive income over time.
Insurance: Get insurance to protect against uncertainties like health emergencies and secure your family's financial future.
Emergency Fund: Keep 3-6 months of living expenses in an emergency fund to handle unforeseen situations without financial strain.

Stats: Over 50% of Indian households rely on informal savings, and less than 20% have health insurance. Only 12% of the working population saves for retirement.

Emergency Fund

Invest your emergency fund in safe instruments like Fixed Deposits (FDs) offering 7-9% returns.

  1. Invest in FDs: Choose safe instruments.
  2. Account for Inflation: Consider 5-7% inflation rates.
  3. Earn Returns: Ensure your fund grows over time.

Note: ₹73,000 crore FDs are unclaimed, highlighting the need to track investments.

Insurance: Term and Health Coverage

Term Insurance

Provides financial security for your family in case of your passing.

Health Insurance

Covers hospitalization and treatment costs, protecting you from medical emergencies.

Stat: Less than 20% of India's population has health insurance.

Investments

Choose investments based on your risk appetite.

Equity: High-risk investments with potential for high returns.
Real Estate: Invest in residential or commercial properties.
Gold: Safe haven offering protection during economic instability.

Stat: Only 3% of Indians invest in stock markets.

Financial Hazards to Avoid

High EMI Burdens: Strain your budget.
Credit Card Debt: High interest charges.
Instant Loan Apps: Predatory lending practices.
Online Betting: Risk of loss and addiction.

Warren Buffett's Five Great Principles

  1. If you buy things you don't need, you will soon sell things you need.
  2. Price is what you pay, and value is what you get.
  3. Never put all your eggs in one basket.
  4. Save first and spend second.
  5. Pay taxes on time for peace of mind.
